Opportunity Classes (OC) in New South Wales

A Guide for Parents and Students

Opportunity Classes (often called OC classes) are specialised, full‐time classes found in many NSW government primary schools. They are designed for intellectually gifted and high‐potential students who demonstrate advanced abilities compared to their age peers. Entry is determined by a placement test that is typically administered when a student is in Year 4, with successful candidates entering the opportunity class in Year 5 and continuing until Year 6.

The Opportunity Class Placement Test is designed to assess a student's ability rather than curriculum knowledge. It is computer‐based and comprises of three key sections, each contributing equally toward the overall test score:

  • Reading Test
    • Content: Questions are based on various texts including non fiction, fiction, poetry, magazine articles, and reports.
    • Format: Approximately 13 questions (note that some questions have multiple parts).
    • Duration: 30 minutes
    • Weighting: 33.3%
  • Mathematical Reasoning Test
    • Content: Assesses the ability to solve problems using mathematical understanding. The questions span different topics, and no calculators or other electronic aids are allowed.
    • Format: 35 multiple choice questions.
    • Duration: 40 minutes
    • Weighting: 33.3%
  • Thinking Skills Test
    • Content: Focuses on logical reasoning, problem-solving, and critical thinking.
    • Format: 30 multiple choice questions.
    • Duration: 30 minutes
    • Weighting: 33.3%

A typical test day for the OC placement test involves a clearly structured timetable. While exact times may vary slightly by test centre, below is a representative schedule (based on recent guidelines):

  • Arrival and Registration
    • Time: Students arrive at the test centre between 8:45 AM and 9:00 AM.
    • Note: Parents/carers should ensure prompt drop off as late arrivals may not be granted extra time.
  • Test Instructions and Administration
    • Time: Approximately 9:00 AM – 9:30 AM
    • Activities: Students are seated, given instructions, and prepared for the computer based test (or provided with the paper-based test where applicable).
  • Reading Test
    • Time: Approximately 9:30 AM – 10:00 AM (30 minutes)
  • Short Break
    • Time: Approximately 10:00 AM – 10:10 AM
    • Activities: Students may remain seated or briefly use the restroom (as permitted).
  • Mathematical Reasoning Test
    • Time: Approximately 10:10 AM – 10:50 AM (40 minutes)
  • Short Break
    • Time: Approximately 10:50 AM – 11:00 AM
  • Thinking Skills Test
    • Time: Approximately 11:00 AM – 11:30 AM (30 minutes)
  • Dismissal
    • Time: Around 11:40 AM – 12 PM
    • Note: Parents/carers should return to pick up their child at the designated time and location indicated on the Test Admission Ticket.

(A sample timetable is provided in OC information documents education.nsw.gov.au.)

Parents should prepare their child with all the necessary items so that the testing environment is as stress free as possible. On test day, students are required to bring:

  • Mandatory Items:
    • Two 2B pencils
    • An eraser
    • A pencil sharpener
    • A printed copy of the Test Admission Ticket (downloaded from the application dashboard)
    • A clear water bottle
    • School uniform (with suitable extras such as a hat or jumper if needed for weather conditions)
  • Optional/Adjustment Items (if approved):
    • Any disability approved adjustments (for example, FM transmitters or other assistive devices as specified)
    • Personal medications (e.g., EpiPen, asthma inhalers, diabetes equipment), glasses, or tissues
    • A wristwatch that does not make any noise or other functions (the official time in the test room is monitored by the test centre clock)
  • Items That Are NOT Allowed:
    • Pens, rulers, note paper, dictionaries, or any electronic devices (including smartwatches, mobile phones, or pencil cases)

  • Travel: Arrange reliable transportation so your child can be dropped off on time (or picked up) at the designated test centre.
  • Supervision: Students are not supervised before the arrival time, so ensure your child has a safe waiting arrangement.
  • Test Centre Environment: The test is administered in external centres (often in local schools) with controlled conditions to ensure a fair testing environment.
  • Health Considerations: Do not bring your child to the test if they are unwell, especially if they exhibit symptoms of contagious illness. If unforeseen circumstances occur (e.g., sudden illness), parents should follow the provided procedures for submitting an illness or misadventure request.

To help your child feel confident and comfortable on test day:

  • Practice Tests: Encourage your child to use the online computer based practice tests as well as PDF practice test copies available on the NSW Department of Education website. Familiarity with the format and question style will help reduce test anxiety.
  • Study with CS Resources: Use the resources we have provided from either WRMT and/or OC to retain knowledge and build familiarity with question types.
  • Normal Routine: Ensure your child gets a good night's sleep the evening before and eats a healthy, balanced breakfast on the day.
  • Discuss the Process: Talk through what the day will involve so your child knows what to expect.

Here are a few targeted tips tailored to each section of the OC Placement Test:

  • Reading Test:
    • Tip 1: Practice reading different types of texts and summarise the main idea in a few sentences. Additionally revise with any CS resources given to you.
    • Tip 2: Look for keywords in each passage; this helps when answering multiple-choice questions.
    • Tip 3: Practice managing time so that questions you find easier are quickly answered, leaving extra time for more complex passages.
  • Mathematical Reasoning Test:
    • Tip 1: Familiarise your child with word problems and real life math scenarios, practice mental calculations. Additionally revise with any CS resources given to you.
    • Tip 2: Remind them that no calculators are allowed; encourage estimation and checking work carefully.
    • Tip 3: Work through sample problems together to build strategies for solving different question types.
    • Tip 4: Practice managing time so that questions you find easier are quickly answered, leaving extra time for more complex questions.
  • Thinking Skills Test:
    • Tip 1: Engage your child with simple puzzles and logic games to sharpen their problem solving skills. Additionally revise with any CS resources given to you.
    • Tip 2: Encourage them to eliminate clearly wrong answers when unsure of the correct choice.
    • Tip 3: Practice flagging questions to return to if time allows; this builds good test management habits.
    • Tip 4: Practice managing time so that questions you find easier are quickly answered, leaving extra time for more complex questions.
  • General Test-Taking Tips:
    • Arrive Early: Ensure your child has plenty of time to settle in before the test starts.
    • Stay Calm: Deep breathing and positive self-talk can help maintain focus.
    • Follow Instructions: Advise your child to read all instructions carefully and to ask for help (by raising their hand) if something is unclear.
    • Time Management: Practice pacing so that no section is rushed, and every question receives proper attention.

Note: Remember your child can not ask for any assistance on how to solve any questions or for meanings.

Opportunity Classes offer a unique environment where gifted and high‐potential students can thrive, learn with peers of similar ability, and be challenged beyond the standard curriculum. With the right preparation, your child can approach test day with confidence. Remember that the placement test is only one part of the overall process and if your child doesn't make it, then it is NOT the end of the line. There are many opportunities ahead to come like Selective and so on.

For more detailed information and updates, parents are encouraged to contact CS Girraween or visit the official NSW Department of Education website and the relevant resource hubs.
